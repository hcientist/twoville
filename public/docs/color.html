Color

You've made a circle that was colored cornflower and a rectangle that was colored crimson. Did you try using other colors?

Many colors are named: :red, :yellow, :magenta, :blue, and so on. Not all of them are named, however. You can get at these unnamed colors using a color list.

Color lists are mixtures of three primary colors: red, green, and blue. They are lists of three intensities, having the form [red, green, blue], where red, green, and blue are numbers between 0 and 1. An intensity of 0 means that the corresponding primary color is fully absent from the mixed color. An intensity of 1 means the primary is fully present.

The symbol :red is shorthand for [1, 0, 0]. The symbol :green is shorthand for [0, 1, 0]. The symbol :blue is shorthand for [0, 0, 1].

To mix together a lot of red, some green, and no blue, we'd write [1, 0.5, 0]. What color is the circle that uses this list?

with circle() radius = 40 center = [50, 50] color = [1, 0.5, 0]

Color lists are something that you should experiment with. Try setting the three intensities in various ways to see what you can you make. What color list is :black short for? What color list is :white short for? How about :yellow? How can you make a light gray? A dark gray?
